What To Do?

Salar d'Uyuni

This place is the largest salt desert of the world - offering you one of the most authentical experience of Bolivia. The immensity of these salt crystals would amaze you. Spending 3 days in this desert won't even feel enought!
Trust us when we assert this experience will be an unforgettable one!

National Reserve Eduardo Avaroa

Sand and salt deserts, lagoons, geysers, and countless natural wonders are waiting for you in the Eduardo Avaroa National Reserve.
You won't regret the experience!

Yungaz Road

Ready for high sensations while cycling through incredible landscapes? The Yungas Road is an ancient Bolivian road transformed into a cycle path linking La Paz to Coroico. This road is 80 kilometres long. His northern section was previously called the "Death Route".
Ready for the challenge?.

Gustu

Gustu is the quechua word meaning "savor". Easy to assert this restaurant serving latin american food is a necessary step during your trip in La Paz.

Gattopardo

Craving for a unique experience in the middle of your adventures? Gattopardo is an amazing place to share unforgettable meals while enjoying a warming decoration in the center of Tarija.

Choriceria "7 Lunares"

Looking for a treat ?
Choriceria is the place to be to eat delicious chorizos as a snack - or (why not?) as a main meal!

Diesel Nacional

Looking forward to party and enjoy every bit of your lifenight in Bolivia? Located in La Paz, Diesel Nacional is the place to be for nice food, affordable cocktails, great meetings, and lifetime experience.

Kultur Berlin Hostel

In the center of Sucre, the Kultur Berlin hostel is known to be a place of fun and joy. This wonderful destination offers you daily events and the best parties in town. And last but not least : they got multiple awards as the best breakfast place in Latin America. Don't think twice before booking!

Bymz Bar

Interested in crazy music as well as great atmosphere to party all night? Bymz Bar is there to welcome you while making out-of-ordinary cocktails.
Do not go through Santa Cruz without making a little jump there!
